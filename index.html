About

Movement as a way of thinking

A body moving through space is already doing geometry.

My doctoral research explores movement- and dance-based learning environments as sites of mathematical work. I study how teachers develop professional vision — the capacity to see mathematical structure in movement, through embodied processing that lets mathematical thinking emerge. Teachers then draw on this acquired vision to design for, and research, their own students' embodied learning.
